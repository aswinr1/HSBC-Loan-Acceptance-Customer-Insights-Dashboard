# 📊 HSBC Personal Loan Dashboard — README

## 🧩 Overview
This dashboard analyzes customer behavior and loan acceptance patterns using HSBC’s personal loan dataset. It highlights key drivers of loan conversion and provides actionable insights for marketing and risk teams.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Identify factors influencing loan acceptance.  
- Segment customers based on financial and behavioral attributes.  
- Support data‑driven decision‑making for targeted campaigns.

---

## 📈 Key Insights
1. **Loan Acceptance by TD Account**  
   Customers with TD accounts show higher acceptance (71%) vs. non‑holders (29%).  
   → Relationship banking strongly drives conversion.

2. **Income, Mortgage, and Credit Card Usage**  
   Higher income, larger mortgage, and higher credit card spending correlate with stronger acceptance.  
   → Financial maturity and spending behavior predict borrowing propensity.

3. **Age and Securities Account Ownership**  
   Younger securities account holders spend more on credit cards; gap narrows with age.  
   → Indicates financial activity among younger demographics.

4. **Income Category Frequency**  
   Majority fall in 0–100K income range; high‑income customers show stronger acceptance.  
   → Mid‑income customers dominate, but high‑income are prime targets.

5. **Education and Online Banking**  
   Graduate and professional users with online banking show higher acceptance.  
   → Education and digital adoption boost loan uptake.

6. **Family Size and Income Influence**  
   Acceptance balanced across family sizes; families of 2 and 4 slightly higher.  
   → Family size alone isn’t decisive but refines segmentation when combined with income.

7. **Cross‑Product Ownership Effect**  
   Acceptance rises sharply with multiple product holdings (up to 57%).  
   → Cross‑selling opportunities are strong among multi‑product customers.

8. **Channel Preference**  
   Digital channel users show 38% acceptance vs. 15% for branch‑only.  
   → Digital engagement enhances conversion.

9. **Regional Segmentation**  
   South region leads with 18% acceptance; West lags at 9%.  
   → Regional targeting can improve campaign efficiency.

10. **Customer Tenure**  
    Long‑tenured customers (7+ years) show 13% acceptance vs. 5% for new customers.  
    → Relationship duration builds trust and credit confidence.

---

## 🧮 Metrics Used
- **Acceptance Rate** = (Sum of LoanFlag ÷ Count of CustomerID) × 100  
- **KPIs:** Acceptance %, Income Group Distribution, Mortgage Value, Credit Card Spend, Education Level, Channel Usage, Tenure.

---

## 🧠 Tools & Techniques
- Excel Pivot Tables and Calculated Fields  
- Acceptance Rate formula and dynamic chart overlays  
- Visuals: Bar, Line, Pie, and Map charts  
- Insights summarized for business interpretation

---

## 🚀 Recommended Actions
- Target **TD account holders** and **digital users** with personalized loan offers.  
- Promote **cross‑product bundles** to increase conversion.  
- Focus marketing on **high‑income and long‑tenured** customers.  
- Expand **regional campaigns** in high‑potential areas (South, Central).  
- Encourage **online banking adoption** among undergraduates.
